• Stimulus 101

  • What's in the stimulus bill?

    Key provisions of the bill include the $1,400 direct payments President Joe Biden has proposed, along with state and local aid, including money for vaccine distribution programs and schools.

    Democrats are also eyeing a slew of additional measures, such as raising the federal minimum wage , a national family leave program, a boost in federal unemployment benefits and a child poverty reduction plan.

  • How long will it take to pass?

    Democrats must pass the bill before March 14 to prevent some boosted unemployment benefits from expiring. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi aims to have the bill finished for a House floor vote on the week of February 22.

    But before that happens, the bill needs to actually be written, a task that falls on 25 committees across both the House and Senate to accomplish over the coming weeks.

  • What's reconciliation? Why does it help Democrats?

    Congress approved a budget resolution giving Democrats the ability to utilize the budget manuever known as reconciliation.

    Budget reconciliation allows passage of a bill in the Senate with just a simple majority of votes. It accomplishes this by preventing the minority party from stalling the bill through a filibuster. Since the Senate is split 50-50, Vice President Kamala Harris' vote gives Democrats a one-vote simple majority. The impact: If Senate Democrats can all agree on a plan, they theoretically don't need any GOP buy-in to pass it.
